Paintings

These are paintings I created in 2D Design and Painting I at Lincoln Land Community College. I took 2D Design in the Fall of 2018 and Painting I in the Fall of 2019. I hadn't done much painting before 2D Design, so I was just learning how in that class.

Complimentary Painting

This is a painting of a red weight on a green cloth. This was my first painting in Painting I. It is a still-life of objects with complimentary colors. This piece was painted on paper.

Triadic Painting

This is a painting of a yellow pear, a red pot lid, and a blue glass bottle. This was my second painting in Painting I. It is another still-life, but this one is of objects with the triadic primary colors. It was painted on canvas that I stretched myself.

Microbiology

This is a painting of microscopic, biological cells or bacteria. To be honest, I don't know for sure what they are, but the painting was inspired by multiple images of microbiology. I made it in Painting I. It was painted on a wood panel.

Still-Life Final

This is a painting of an ornament, a green box, a baseball cap, and a candle. It was my final project for Painting I. I had the choice to paint anything for the final, so I chose to retry painting a still-life. I wanted to make something like my triadic painting but better, using the techniques I had learned throughout the rest of the semester.

Magazine Cutouts

This is a painting based on cutouts from a magazine. I made it in 2D Design. First, I cut out pieces of a magazine and glued them to a piece of paper to make a design. Then, I attempted to paint the magazine cutouts. I was still learning to paint during this time.

Static Painting

This is a painting of asymmetrical squares. I made it in 2D Design. It was made to show the asymmetrical and static elements of design.

Symmetry Painting

This is a painting of a geometric design with radial symmetry. I made it in 2D Design. The assignment was for us to show that we know the symmetrical and geometric elements of design.

The Color Wheel

This is a painting showing colors' tints, shades, and levels of saturation; there's also a color wheel I painted on the side. I made it in 2D Design. This was one of my first painted pieces. I made it when I was learning the properties of color and learning how to mix paint.
